# Product Requirements Document (PRD)
## Project: thought-bridge
## Version: 1.0
## Date: 2026-06-22

## Problem Statement
AI-powered tools often prioritize speed over correctness, leading to inaccurate or unreliable results. This can result in wasted time, resources, and even harm to users. The thought-bridge tool aims to address this issue by providing accurate and reliable high-level thinking and low-level implementation for AI-powered tools.

## Target Users
- **Primary Users**: Developers and engineers working on AI-powered projects, who require accurate and reliable results to ensure the quality of their products.
- **Secondary Users**: Researchers and scientists who rely on accurate and reliable AI-powered tools for their work.

## Goals
- Provide accurate and reliable high-level thinking for AI-powered tools.
- Ensure correctness over speed in AI-powered tool implementation.
- Reduce the risk of inaccurate or unreliable results in AI-powered tools.

## Key Features (Prioritized)
### High Priority
1. **Correctness Verification**: Implement a correctness verification system to ensure that AI-powered tools produce accurate and reliable results.
2. **High-Level Thinking**: Develop a high-level thinking module that provides accurate and reliable results for AI-powered tools.
3. **Low-Level Implementation**: Provide a low-level implementation module that ensures correctness over speed in AI-powered tool implementation.

### Medium Priority
1. **Integration with Popular AI Frameworks**: Integrate thought-bridge with popular AI frameworks to expand its reach and usability.
2. **User-Friendly Interface**: Develop a user-friendly interface for thought-bridge to simplify its usage and adoption.
3. **Scalability**: Ensure that thought-bridge can scale to handle large and complex AI-powered projects.

### Low Priority
1. **Advanced Features**: Develop advanced features such as real-time feedback and optimization for thought-bridge.
2. **Multi-Language Support**: Add support for multiple programming languages to thought-bridge.

## Success Metrics
- **Accuracy**: Measure the accuracy of thought-bridge's results compared to human experts.
- **Reliability**: Measure the reliability of thought-bridge's results over time.
- **Adoption**: Measure the number of users and projects that adopt thought-bridge.

## Scope
- The scope of thought-bridge includes providing accurate and reliable high-level thinking and low-level implementation for AI-powered tools.
- The scope excludes developing AI-powered tools from scratch, but rather focuses on improving the accuracy and reliability of existing tools.

## Out-of-Scope
- Developing AI-powered tools from scratch.
- Providing support for non-AI powered tools.
- Developing advanced features that are not critical to the core functionality of thought-bridge.

## Assumptions and Dependencies
- The thought-bridge tool assumes that the AI-powered tools it integrates with are properly configured and maintained.
- The thought-bridge tool depends on the availability of high-quality training data for its high-level thinking module.

## Risks and Mitigation Strategies
- **Risk**: Thought-bridge may not be able to accurately and reliably verify the correctness of AI-powered tools.
- **Mitigation Strategy**: Continuously test and validate thought-bridge's results against human experts to ensure accuracy and reliability.

## Next Steps
- Develop the correctness verification system.
- Implement the high-level thinking module.
- Provide a low-level implementation module.
